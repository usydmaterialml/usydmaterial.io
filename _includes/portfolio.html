Machine learning for better carbon dioxide hydrogenation catalysts
There is little question that the sustainable reductive transformation of CO2 is one of the most important scientific and technical challenges of the 21st century. Machine learning methods have proven successful in the prediction of a large number of material properties. However, there is still a number of challenges in computational material sci-ences and there is a need for novel strategies to overcome them. Catalytic CO2 hydrogenation is one of the most important prototype reactions in heterogeneous catalysis, and the current big challenge is the lack of low-cost, highly selective, active and stable catalysts to synthesize valuable C2 chemicals. This theme will assess the per-formance of existing methods for predicting the performance of carbon dioxide hydrogenation catalysts, and devel-op new ideas to accelerate the engineering and design of complex materials with improved functionalities.
